How The Work Evolved

OpenAssistant

OpenAssistant was the first one. I wanted OpenAI's Assistants API on my phone badly enough that I built the client myself. It got rejected around 30 times before it finally shipped, which taught me App Review the hard way.

OpenCone

Then I wanted retrieval on my phone, not just chat. OpenCone became the app where I figured out how document ingestion, embeddings, and real mobile RAG workflows were actually supposed to feel.

OpenResponses

When Assistants got deprecated, I rebuilt around the Responses API and full tool orchestration in OpenResponses. By then I had learned enough from the first two apps that this one passed App Review on the first submission.

OpenIntelligence

After WWDC25, I wanted to build something proprietary and fully mine. OpenIntelligence became an offline Apple Intelligence RAG app with three quality modes. The hardest part was the 4,096-token ceiling on the on-device 3B model, so I built a recursive reasoning loop to push past it and keep the answers long, grounded, and cited.

Supporting Systems

Outside the App Store apps, I build supporting systems and prototypes like PlaudBlender and MedMod whenever I need to understand a workflow deeply enough to challenge it.

What started as curiosity turned into a pattern: learn the system, build through the constraints, and keep going until it feels solid.

OpenResponses

OpenResponses is where I stopped just proving I could build these apps and started hardening them like a real product.

  • Why it exists: When OpenAI deprecated the Assistants API, I rebuilt around the Responses API instead of letting the old app rot.
  • What it does: Native iOS client for tool-enabled chat with web search, file search, code interpreter, image generation, and MCP connectivity.
  • What it taught me: After the App Review pain of the first two apps, this one passed on the first submission because I finally knew where the landmines were.
  • Who it is for: People who want serious API control on mobile instead of a black-box chat wrapper.

OpenIntelligence

OpenIntelligence is the first app that felt fully mine: an offline Apple Intelligence RAG engine built to run in airplane mode.

  • Origin: I started building it after WWDC25 because Apple finally exposed enough of the hardware and on-device model stack to make something new possible.
  • Core system: Local ingestion, hybrid retrieval, and three quality modes: Standard, Deep Think, and Maximum.
  • Hard problem: The on-device 3B model hit a 4,096-token ceiling, so I built a recursive reasoning loop to push past it and keep answers long, cited, and grounded.
  • Why it matters: This is the app that made me think I should be doing this on a much larger scale.

OpenCone

OpenCone came from a simpler urge: I wanted RAG on my phone, not just another chat app.

  • Why it exists: I wanted a real mobile retrieval workflow where documents, context, and answers could all live in one place.
  • What it does: Takes documents from the file picker through local extraction and chunking into Pinecone-backed retrieval and OpenAI-powered answers.
  • What it taught me: This is where I learned what ingestion pipelines, retrieval tradeoffs, and document workflows actually demand on mobile.
  • Why it still matters: It was the second app in the sequence and a major step in turning curiosity into a repeatable build process.
