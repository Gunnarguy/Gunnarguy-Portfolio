About Me

I'm Gunnar - someone who's taken an unconventional path to software engineering. Currently, I work in the Operating Room as a Medical Device Specialist, providing intraoperative technical support where there's no safety net and surgeons depend on the tech I manage. But my real transformation began in March 2023 when GPT-4 changed everything.

My journey wasn't linear. I started wanting to be a cop, pivoted to exercise science, worked as a utility locator for four years, and eventually found my calling at Stryker. Each pivot taught me something crucial: I needed work where I could control outcomes, solve real problems, and make systems better.

When AI became accessible, it didn't give me motivation—it unlocked what was already there. I taught myself iOS development, shipped two apps to the App Store, built Python automation systems, and created tools that surgical teams actually use. I don't build for show. I build because I see the problem and can't not solve it.

I learn by doing, debug my way through ambiguity, and thrive when given worthy problems. When I'm not coding, you can find me cycling through scenic landscapes, reading Isaac Asimov and Philip K. Dick, or enjoying modern dance and theatre performances.
