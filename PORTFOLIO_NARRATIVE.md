# Portfolio Narrative

## Core Story

Gunnar is not coming to software from the outside. He works inside high-variability surgical environments where uptime, logistics, personalities, and technical reliability all collide in real time.

By day, he provides intraoperative technical support to Stanford surgeons at the VA in Palo Alto as Stryker's on-site specialist. There is no local management layer buffering the work. He is the first line of defense for keeping the contract healthy across equipment readiness, turnover reliability, procurement pressure, repair and replacement coordination, stakeholder relationships, and day-to-day operational variability.

By night, he builds software the same way he solves problems at work: learn the system fast, read the documentation obsessively, identify the weak points, and close the gaps until the thing feels real. The strongest expression of that is four shipped App Store apps built in Swift and SwiftUI.

## Why OpenIntelligence Matters

OpenIntelligence is the most personal and defensible project. It is the one that moved the story from "I can build apps" to "I want to do this at scale." Key details to preserve:

- It started after WWDC25 and the Apple Silicon / Apple Intelligence unlocks
- It runs fully offline / in airplane mode
- It has three quality modes: Standard, Deep Think, and Maximum
- The hardest problem was the 4,096 token ceiling on the on-device 3B model
- Gunnar built a recursive reasoning loop to push past that limit while keeping responses long, cited, and accurate

## Voice Guardrails

- Use concrete nouns instead of abstract positioning language
- Use counts, stakes, and responsibility wherever possible
- Avoid generic AI phrasing unless it is grounded immediately by specifics
- Avoid making every repo sound equally important
- Prefer lived detail over polished slogans
- Let App Review failures and progression stay in the story; they make it real
- Keep the through-line on autonomy, curiosity, and workflow obsession
